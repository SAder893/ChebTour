Guide of Cheboksary City

Basic information.

Cheboksary is a city in Russia, the capital of the Chuvash Republic, its administrative, economic and cultural center. Cheboksary consists of three administrative districts — Kalininsky, Leninsky, Moskovsky and Zavolzhsky territorial administration. It is a river port on the right bank of the Volga River, at the confluence of the Cheboksary River.

The name comes from the Cheboksary River, to the mouth of which the historical part of the city adjoins. Cheboksary area: over 250 km2. The federal highway M-7 Volga passes through the city, connecting it with Moscow, Kazan, Novgorod, Vladimir, Ufa. The distance between Cheboksary and Moscow is about 660 km. The climate is temperate continental. The majority of the population are Chuvash. Many national traditions and language are preserved in the city — it is taught at school, all city signs and signs are duplicated in Chuvash.

By decree of the President of the Russian Federation dated May 20, 2021, the city was awarded the title "City of Labor Valor." Cheboksary was also awarded the Order of the Red Banner of Labor.


The history of Cheboksary.

The first settlements on the site of modern Cheboksary date back to the 13th–14th centuries, but the official founding date of the city is considered to be May 1469. After the collapse of the Golden Horde, the territory became part of the Kazan Khanate, and in 1551 the Chuvash and the Mountain Mari peacefully joined Russia. Since 1555, a fortress was built here to protect the southern borders, and Cheboksary district was created.

By the end of the 17th century, the city had become an important trading center on the Volga. In the 18th century, stone buildings and churches were built here, and Catherine II admired its beauty. At the beginning of the 19th century, the population was about 5.5 thousand people, and industry was limited.

After the October Revolution Cheboksary became an important economic and cultural center. In 1920, the city became the capital of the Chuvash Autonomous Region, and during the war years it worked as a rear city, where local residents and evacuees from the western regions worked. Post-Soviet changes have contributed to the recovery and development of new areas, including tourism. In the 21st century, intensive construction of new structures, houses, cultural centers, industrial and agricultural enterprises began.

Today Cheboksary is a modern city with a rich historical heritage, attracting tourists with its architecture and cultural events.


Cheboksary attractions

1. Red Square

Cheboksary's Red Square is one of the oldest squares in the capital of the Chuvash Republic. It is located in the city center, in the Leninsky district.


Before the revolution, the square had other dimensions and was called Bazarnaya. Plekhanov and Nizhegorodskaya Streets ran through the square, but after the construction of the hydroelectric power station, most of this area of the city was under water, and the remaining buildings were incorporated into the newly formed square. In Soviet times, the Market Square underwent a global restructuring and was renamed Krasnaya. Later, the N. K. Krupskaya Park and the Children's Park adjacent to the square also became part of the square. Previously, Plekhanov Street passed through this territory, but when Cheboksary Bay was filled in 1996, parts of the streets were under water, and the remaining buildings along Plekhanov Street were subsequently incorporated into Red Square.

Modern dimensions: length — 400 m, width — 150 m. It is bounded by Cheboksary Bay from the north and west, Yaroslavskaya Street from the east, which turns into a transport and pedestrian dam, and capital buildings from the south.

On the square there are: a monument to the Chuvash poet K. V. Ivanov, the Alley of Champions with the names of outstanding athletes of Chuvashia. Among the buildings on the square: the Chuvash State Academic Drama Theater, the Center for Contemporary Art (Museum), the Chuvash National Museum, the building of the Institute of Advanced Medical Training, an administrative building, several cafes and a restaurant on the bay shore.

The square is a popular recreation area for citizens and attracts many visitors to the city. On Cheboksary City Day and Chuvash Republic Day, the square is one of the main venues for large-scale celebrations.

Address: Cheboksary, Red Square.

2. Vvedensky Cathedral

Vvedensky Cathedral is an Orthodox church in Cheboksary, the cathedral of the Chuvash Metropolia and Cheboksary diocese of the Russian Orthodox Church.

The main altar of the church was consecrated in honor of the feast of the Entry of the Most Holy Theotokos into the temple. The first (log) temple on this site was founded according to the charter of Tsar Ivan the Terrible dated May 26, 1555. The current building was built in the middle of the 17th century (in the 1650s or 1660s).

>

In Soviet times, the temple was closed for several years. By a decree of the Government of Chuvashia in 1939, its building was transferred to the newly formed Chuvash State Art Gallery. In 1943, divine services in the cathedral were resumed. The interiors of the cathedral have preserved the frescoes of the Classical era, as well as the iconostasis. The tent-roofed bell tower is typical of 17th-century Moscow architecture.

There are a lot of rare icons in the temple. But the most important of them is Our Lady of Vladimir. It was this icon that was presented to the temple by Gury the Wonderworker of Kazan in the 15th century. It is also worth paying attention to the ancient frescoes made in the 17th and 18th centuries, and the iconostasis.

Address: Cheboksary, Konstantin Ivanov St., 21.

3. The Chuvash National Museum

The Chuvash National Museum in Cheboksary is the largest repository of monuments of nature, history, material and spiritual culture of the Chuvash and other ethnic groups. The museum tells about the culture, history and traditions of the Chuvash people through a variety of exhibits.

The museum was founded in 1921 thanks to the efforts of the local intelligentsia under the leadership of Nikolai Pavlovich Neverov. Over the years, the museum has changed its status and location several times: in 1937 it became the Chuvash Central Museum of Local Lore, and in 1993 it received the status of a national museum.

The museum's collections number more than 212 thousand exhibits covering various historical and cultural aspects of Chuvashia. The largest collection is the archaeological one, which contains about 40 thousand artifacts. The museum's exhibitions cover several thematic areas, such as nature, everyday life and culture, history and ethnography. The hall "Nature and Man" features stuffed animals, minerals and herbaria.

The museum offers educational programs, guided tours, workshops and quests for visitors of all ages. The museum actively cooperates with researchers, organizes scientific conferences and readings.

Address: Cheboksary, Red Square, 5/2.

4. Victory Memorial Park

The Victory Memorial Park in Cheboksary is dedicated to the memory of soldiers who did not return from the fronts of the Great Patriotic War, Afghan soldiers, and participants in the Chechen conflicts.

The park is located at the highest point of Cheboksary on the banks of the Volga River in the city center and occupies more than 30 hectares.

On the territory of the park there are:

— Monument of Military Glory. It is a composition made of bronze: the Motherland holds a banner and points forward, a soldier kneels next to her with a weapon in his hands.

— The Walk of Fame. The main avenue is made of blue fir trees, and it leads to the Monument of Military Glory and the Eternal Flame.

— Museum of Military Equipment. More than a dozen samples of military equipment have been collected in this place, including an airplane, helicopter, armored vehicles and artillery.

— St. John's Memorial Chapel. It is a place of remembrance and mourning for the fallen soldiers.

Address: Cheboksary, Alley of Heroes.

5. V. I. Chapaev Museum

The V. I. Chapaev Museum in Cheboksary is a branch of the Chuvash National Museum, a scientific and educational institution of the Chuvash Republic.

He studies, preserves and shows the events of the Civil War period using the example of the life and work of V. I. Chapaev, his military associates and political opponents.

In 1986, the Chapaev family house was restored next to the museum. On February 9, 1887, the future commander was born in this house in the suburban village of Budaika. It recreates the interior of a Russian peasant hut at the end of the 19th century, using authentic household items and utensils belonging to V.I. Chapaev's relatives from the villages of Budaika and Gremyachevo.

The museum complex includes Chapaev Square, the museum building, the Chapaev Memorial house, a monument to V. I. Chapaev, a memorial wall with bas-reliefs by D. A. Furmanov, M. V. Frunze, M. N. Tukhachevsky, V. V. Kuibyshev, I. S. Kutyakov.

The museum's exposition tells about Vasily Ivanovich's childhood, the periods of the First World War and the Civil War, his studies at the Academy of the General Staff, and the military operations in which military units created by Chapaev participated.

Address: Cheboksary, Lenin ave., 46A (Chapaev Square).

6. Chuvash Drama Theater named after K. V. Ivanov

The Chuvash State Academic Drama Theater named after K. V. Ivanov is the oldest theater in the Chuvash Republic, one of the first national theaters in the country.

It was founded in 1918 in Kazan by I. S. Maksimov-Koshkinsky. In 1920 he moved to Cheboksary.

On January 17, 1933, by a decree of the Presidium of the Central Executive Committee of the CHASSR, the theater was awarded the title of “academic”. In 1959– it was named after Konstantin Vasilyevich Ivanov. By decree of the Presidium of the Supreme Soviet of the USSR dated December 29, 1977, he was awarded the Order of the Red Banner of Labor for his services to the development of theatrical art.

The theater's repertoire consisted of plays by Chuvash playwrights and translations of Russian classics, and performances were performed in the Chuvash language. In 1933, the theater was awarded the title of academic, and in 1959 — the name of Konstantin Vasilyevich Ivanov.

The modern theater building was built in 1961 by architect Alexander Maksimov. The building is a historical and cultural monument of local importance.

The theater's repertoire includes performances based on both classical and modern works of Russian and Chuvash drama. The performances are mostly in Chuvash with simultaneous translation into Russian. The children's repertoire and fairy tales are in Russian.

Address: Cheboksary, Red Square, 7.

7. Monument to the Patron Mother

The monument "Mother Patroness" in Cheboksary is located in the historical part of the city, on an ancient hill adjacent to Cheboksary Bay from the west.

The monument is considered one of the main attractions not only of Cheboksary, but also of the whole of Chuvashia. This sculpture is dedicated to all mothers who protect their children.

Dimensions: on a 25-meter pedestal stands a 16-meter female figure, the total height of the monument is 46 meters.

The sculpture depicts a woman of Chuvash nationality in traditional clothes with her arms outstretched to indicate a gesture of hugging, blessing and protection.

The inscription on the pedestal is written in two languages — Russian and Chuvash: "Blessed are my children who live in peace and love."

The author of the idea is the President of Chuvashia Nikolai Fedorov, and the sculptor Vladimir Nagornov embodied the idea. The monument was unveiled on May 9, 2003.

Address: Cheboksary, Historical embankment, 14.

8. Russian Drama Theatre

The Russian Drama Theater in Cheboksary is one of the oldest professional theaters in the Chuvash Republic. Founded on December 14, 1922, the first performance was Vasilisa Melentyeva based on the play by A. N. Ostrovsky.

The repertoire includes dramas, comedies, musical performances and fairy tales for children. The theater annually participates in festivals of international and national scale, the troupe tours a lot.

Russian Russian Drama Theater For many years, the activities of the State Russian Drama Theater of the Chuvash Republic have been perceived as a living continuation of the traditions of Russian scenic realism, the high art with which a team of great masters reveals the artistic riches of dramatic classics, passionate civic pathos, and modern plays staged here.

On December 14, 2022, the State Russian Drama Theater celebrated its 100th anniversary, with a gala evening on December 16. To mark the anniversary, the theater released a commemorative album "The Golden Age of the Russian Drama Theater", organized an exhibition "Theater in Faces" and made a film about the theater, which was shown on the National Television of the Chuvash Republic. Russian President Vladimir Putin sent a government telegram congratulating the theater on its anniversary, and on February 27, 2023, the theater staff received the Thanks of Russian President Vladimir Putin.

On January 30, 2024, the Russian Drama Theater, represented by director Dmitry Kapustin, was awarded the Fyodor Volkov Prize of the Ministry of Culture of the Russian Federation.

Address: Cheboksary, Gagarin St., 14.

9. Cheboksary 500th Anniversary Park

Cheboksary 500th Anniversary Park of Culture and Recreation is an open, landscaped and landscaped area designed for recreation and walking.

The park area is 87 hectares.

Cheboksary 500th Anniversary Park, although it is not considered the main recreation place for citizens (this status has been assigned to Lakreevsky Forest), Cheboksary residents who visit the Moskovskaya Embankment area like to walk along its alleys. Located on the high bank of the Volga River in the Chernyshevsky ravine area, part of the city has the most favorable ecological situation.

It was founded in 1969 to mark the 500th anniversary of the city. The opening took place in early 1978. For a long time it was a branch of the central park of culture and recreation "Lakreevsky Forest". In the 90s, there was a period of decline and ruin here. But still, to date, the park has managed to regain its title as a popular holiday destination for Cheboksary residents.

Currently, the Amazonia ethnic complex, which includes various facilities, is located on the territory of the park.

Address: Cheboksary, Amazonia ethnic complex.

10. National Library of the Chuvash Republic

The National Library of the Chuvash Republic is the main cultural and educational center of the republic, the only public repository with a complete collection of Chuvash books and literature about Chuvashia.

Founded in 1871, the fund has more than 2 million items, including about 56 thousand books in the Chuvash language.

In 1918-1920 Cheboksary Public Library was subordinated to Cheboksary district Department of Public Education of Kazan province. During this time, the number of readers increased from 162 to 760, a reading room for 55-60 people was opened, 4 librarians worked in the staff.

Computerization of library and bibliographic processes began in 1991. In 1994, the Gorky Chuvash Republican Library acquired its modern name and received the status of a national library. The status of the national library has allowed us to reach a new level of development: to strengthen the role of the library as the most comprehensive array of national press and literature about the republic, to intensify work on the creation of a national bibliography, to become a methodological center for working with national literature.

In 2021, the year of its 150th anniversary, the National Library of the Chuvash Republic was awarded the Gratitude of the Head of the Chuvash Republic, honorary diplomas of the Chuvash Republic and the Federation Council of the Federal Assembly of the Russian Federation.

Address: Cheboksary, Lenin ave., 15.

11. Tractor History Museum

The Museum of Tractor History in Cheboksary is the only institution in Russia dedicated entirely to agricultural, industrial and construction machinery.

It was established in 2010 on the initiative of the management of the Tractor Plants Concern and with the support of the Russian Engineering Union. The museum received its first visitors on October 29, 2011.

The exposition covers an area of more than 1.5 thousand square meters. m. It gives a complete picture of the history of the formation and development of the Russian and world tractor industry.

The collection includes more than 50 rare wheeled, tracked, industrial, skidding tractors, including such tractor models as Fordson, McCormick Deering, Universal-2, T-28, Porshe, T-330 and others.

The museum also has a collection of 600 collectible models in 1:42 scale of various samples of tractor equipment for agricultural and industrial purposes. Some of them are the result of cooperation between the Tractor History Museum and the French Premium & Collectibles Trading Europe. The collection includes more than 5,000 rare items.

Address: Cheboksary, Prospekt Mira, 1, building 2.

12. Chuvash State Opera and Ballet Theatre

The Chuvash State Opera and Ballet Theater in Cheboksary was established on the basis of the Chuvash State Musical Theater in 1993.

The building was built in 1985 in the style of Soviet modernism on the shore of Cheboksary Bay.

In contrast to the monumental and ascetic facades, the interior has a palatial, solemn character. Lots of marble, wood and metal. There is a grand staircase leading from the main entrance to the central foyer, a large room with 1,000 seats, but a very cozy room, and a nice balcony.

The first production was the opera "Shyvarman" by Fyodor Vasiliev. The theater stages operas by Russian and foreign classics, such as Eugene Onegin, as well as Chuvash folk performances, including Narspi.

The repertoire includes classical ballets and musical fairy tales for children. The theater building, built in 1985, is an example of Soviet modernism. The Maxim Mikhailov International Opera Festival has been held here since 1991, and the annual International Ballet Festival has been held since 1997.

Address: Cheboksary, Moskovsky Prospekt, 1.
